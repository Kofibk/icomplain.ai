I am writing to make a claim under Section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 in relation to a holiday booking that was never provided due to the supplier going out of business.

**The Purchase**

On 20 January 2024, I purchased an all-inclusive family holiday to Turkey from Sunshine Holidays Ltd. The holiday was for 2 weeks for 4 people, including flights and hotel accommodation, scheduled for August 2024.

- **Total cost**: £4,200
- **Amount paid on HSBC credit card**: £500 (deposit)
- **Amount paid by bank transfer**: £3,700

**What Went Wrong**

In June 2024, Sunshine Holidays Ltd suddenly ceased trading. Their website disappeared and their phone lines were disconnected. I never received any communication from them. As a result:
- I did not receive the holiday
- I did not receive the flights
- I did not receive the hotel accommodation
- The company is no longer trading and I cannot contact them

**My Claim Under Section 75**

Section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 makes the credit card provider equally liable with the supplier for any breach of contract or misrepresentation, provided:
- The cash price of the item is between £100 and £30,000
- At least part of the purchase was made using the credit card

In this case:
- The total purchase price was £4,200 (within the qualifying range)
- I paid £500 of this on my HSBC credit card
- There has been a clear breach of contract as the holiday was never provided

**Remedy Sought**

Under Section 75, I am claiming the full purchase price of £4,200. Although I only paid £500 on the credit card, Section 75 provides that the credit card company is jointly and severally liable for the entire amount lost, not just the portion paid on the card.

I am therefore claiming:
- Refund of the full £4,200
